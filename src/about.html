Hey!
I'm James Robinson,
a self-taught
Front End Developer

I am a completely self-taught, front end developer. I do not have a computer science degree, nor have I ever paid for an expensive bootcamp. Before becoming a developer, I've worked as a strength and conditioning coach, a night-shift warehouse worker and I've also pulled pints behind a bar. I've taught myself everything I needed to know using only a glorious little thing called the internet. After finding myself at crossroads in life, I decided on a whim to give coding 'a go'. Over three years later, I haven't looked back.

After just under a year of intensive learning, I managed to bag my first job as a Junior Front End Developer. Now I want to share my journey - helping aspiring front end developers get their first jobs in the industry. Having reflected on what worked well for me (and what didn't), I want to use my own personal learning processes and my own experiences in a real job in tech to help you learn to code and acquire the skills you actually need to get a job.

Launching The Front End Coach

I've been through the process myself. Learnning to code isn't easy, and it's certainly not easy teaching yourself. Knowing where to start, what resources to use and what to learn can become a real headache, fast. Trying to sift your way through the never ending see of guides, tutorials, and blog posts can be off putting and quite frankly, damn right confusing.

I've launched this site and support system to give you my no BS, honest advice and support, helping you to learn only what you need to learn and in the most time efficient way possible, helping to give you every chance at kick-starting your career as a front end developer.

Get Support!
